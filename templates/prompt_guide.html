Mastering AI Prompts for Trading Strategies in NexusTrade AI Lab

Welcome, aspiring AI traders! In the NexusTrade AI Lab, your most powerful tool isn't complex code or arcane financial algorithms; it's your ability to communicate effectively with the AI. This guide will help you craft detailed, effective prompts for your strategies.

Quick Start: Running Your First Backtest

Ready to try your first AI trading strategy? Follow these quick steps to run a basic backtest using our pre-saved data.

Step 1: Input a Simple Strategy

On the Member Dashboard, go to the "Backtesting Mode" section. In the text area, paste a basic prompt like this:

"You are a simple trend-following bot. If the close price is above the SMA_30, BUY. If the close price is below the SMA_30, SELL. Otherwise, HOLD. Provide concise reasoning."

Step 2: Use Our Pre-saved Data

Below the prompt, check the box labeled "Use sample BTC data". This will automatically use a pre-loaded dataset, so you don't need to upload a file.

Step 3: Set a Short Backtest Duration

Set the "Days to Backtest" to 1. Backtesting for longer periods can cause a significant increase in wait times due to the number of AI API calls, so a shorter duration is highly recommended for testing.

Step 4: Run the Backtest

Click the "Run Backtest" button. The "Session Results" area will appear below, showing the status and eventually the performance metrics for your strategy.

That's it! You've run your first AI backtest. Continue reading below to learn how to craft more advanced and powerful prompts.

The Full Guide: Mastering AI Prompts

Your prompt is a natural language instruction that defines the rules, context, and desired output for our AI. It dictates its persona, knowledge base, and decision-making process.

1. Define the AI's Persona and Goal

Start by setting the stage. Clearly tell the AI what its role is and what it's trying to achieve. For example:

"You are a highly experienced and cautious crypto trading bot specializing in Bitcoin (BTC) against USDT. Your main goal is to generate consistent, small profits while strictly managing risk and avoiding large drawdowns."

2. Specify Input Data and How to Interpret It

Tell the AI what kind of data it will receive. The platform provides 1-minute OHLCV (Open, High, Low, Close, Volume) data, along with indicators like SMA (Simple Moving Average), RSI (Relative Strength Index), and ATR (Average True Range). Guide its interpretation:

"Analyze the provided 1-minute candlestick data. The data includes 'open', 'high', 'low', 'close' prices, 'volume', 'sma_30', 'sma_60', 'rsi', and 'atr'. Focus primarily on the 'close' price, its relationship to the 'sma_30' and 'sma_60', and the 'rsi' value. Consider 'volume' as a confirmation factor for price movements."

3. Outline Trading Logic (Conditions for BUY, SELL, HOLD)

This is the core of your strategy. Be as specific as possible about the conditions for each action. Use logical operators (AND, OR) where appropriate.

"**BUY Signal:** Initiate a BUY order if the current 'close' price is above both 'sma_30' and 'sma_60', AND the 'rsi' is below 60. You must have sufficient USDT balance to execute the trade."

4. Incorporate Risk Management

Crucial for real-world (and simulated) trading. Tell the AI how to protect capital.

"Your simulated starting balance is `STARTING_BALANCE_USD` USDT. Do not overtrade. If a BUY signal requires more USDT than you possess, adjust to HOLD. Do not risk more than 2% of your total equity on any single trade."

5. Define Output Format

Tell the AI exactly how you want its response structured. This is vital for the platform to parse the AI's decision.

"Your response must strictly contain three parts: `ACTION`, `CONFIDENCE`, and `REASONING`.
Example: 'BUY 90% RSI crossed up from oversold, volume confirmed breakout.'"

Tips for Iteration and Refinement

Prompt engineering is an iterative process. Here are some tips to refine your strategies:

  • Start Simple: Begin with a basic strategy and gradually add complexity.
  • Be Explicit: Ambiguity is the enemy. Use concrete terms like "greater than or equal to" and "last 5 candles."
  • Use Numeric Values: Whenever possible, use concrete numbers (e.g., `RSI is below 30`) rather than vague terms.
  • Test, Analyze, Adjust: Run backtests, review the AI's "REASONING," and adjust your prompt based on its performance.

Test Prompts

Here are some pre-written prompts you can copy and paste into the backtesting form to see different strategies in action. These range from simple to complex.

1. The Simple Trend Follower (Low Complexity)

A basic strategy that follows a short-term trend based on a single indicator.

"You are a simple trend-following bot. If the close price is above the SMA_30, BUY. If the close price is below the SMA_30, SELL. Otherwise, HOLD. Provide concise reasoning."

2. The Momentum Scalper (Medium Complexity)

A more active strategy that looks for overbought/oversold conditions using RSI and confirms with volume.

"You are a momentum scalper. Your goal is to make quick, small profits. Issue a BUY if the RSI is below 30 and volume has increased in the last 3 minutes. Issue a SELL if the RSI is above 70 and volume is decreasing. HOLD otherwise."

3. The Volatility Breakout Trader (Medium Complexity)

This strategy looks for price breakouts from periods of low volatility, using ATR and SMA as key signals.

"You are a volatility breakout trader. Look for periods where ATR has been low for a sustained period. Issue a BUY if the close price breaks significantly above the SMA_60 when ATR starts to spike. Issue a SELL if the close price breaks significantly below the SMA_60 when ATR starts to spike. In all other cases, HOLD."

4. The Aggressive Scalper (High Complexity)

This prompt is designed to be highly aggressive and aims to overcome the AI's natural risk guard for more frequent trading opportunities.

"You are a highly aggressive, high-risk cryptocurrency scalping bot. Your sole purpose is to find and execute profitable short-term trades. You will prioritize maximizing profit over capital preservation. Do not default to HOLD unless there is no market movement at all. High volatility is an opportunity for profit, not a cause for caution.

You will receive the latest 60 minutes of 1-minute OHLCV data for BTC/USDT, including 'open', 'high', 'low', 'close', 'volume', 'sma_30', 'sma_60', 'rsi', and 'atr'.

**BUY Signal:** Issue a BUY if 'rsi' is below 55 and trending upwards OR if 'close' has crossed above 'sma_30'. Treat any increase in volatility (higher 'atr') as a signal to look for an entry.

**SELL Signal:** Issue a SELL if 'rsi' is above 70 and trending downwards OR if 'close' has crossed below 'sma_30'. Treat any increase in volatility (higher 'atr') as a signal to look for an exit.

**HOLD Signal:** Issue a HOLD only if there has been less than a 0.1% price change in the last 15 minutes. In all other market conditions, you must attempt to find a trading opportunity."

5. The Cautious Investor (High Complexity)

A conservative strategy designed for long-term holds, only entering and exiting based on strong, confirmed signals to minimize risk.

"You are a very cautious long-term investor. You only trade based on strong, confirmed signals to avoid high-risk situations.

You will receive the latest 60 minutes of 1-minute OHLCV data for BTC/USDT, including 'open', 'high', 'low', 'close', 'volume', 'sma_30', 'sma_60', 'rsi', and 'atr'.

**BUY Signal:** Issue a BUY only when the 'close' price has been consistently above both 'sma_30' AND 'sma_60' for at least the last 15 minutes, AND 'rsi' is below 60 and rising. The volume must also be significantly above average to confirm the trend.

**SELL Signal:** Issue a SELL only when the 'close' price has been consistently below both 'sma_30' AND 'sma_60' for at least the last 15 minutes, AND 'rsi' is above 70 and falling. The volume must also be significantly above average to confirm the trend reversal.

**HOLD Signal:** In all other cases, you must HOLD. Your primary goal is capital preservation, not profit maximization."
